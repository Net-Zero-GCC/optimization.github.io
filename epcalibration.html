Workflow

Five steps from measured data to a calibrated model

The full calibration pipeline inside one desktop application.

01

Tag parameters

One-click tag injection for insulation, thermal mass, windows, infiltration, occupancy, lighting and equipment — or place custom tags in the built-in IDF editor.

02

Upload measured data

Load measured CSVs from any monitoring system or utility bill — one dataset per objective, with a time-period filter to align simulation and measurement.

03

Baseline check

Run EnergyPlus once, pick any output variable, and instantly see RMSE, NMBE and CV(RMSE) before optimization starts.

04

NSGA-II search

Halton-seeded NSGA-II explores the parameter space with step grids, evaluation caching and parallel EnergyPlus runs.

05

Pareto exploration

Animate the front across generations, pick the balanced solution, inspect any point's parameters, and export CSV and plots.

Capabilities

Everything you need, in one pipeline

Built for real calibration studies — for practitioners, energy auditors and researchers.

Multi-objective NSGA-II

Calibrate several zones or datasets at once — each objective's RMSE is minimized simultaneously along a true Pareto front.

Single-dataset mode

Only a monthly energy bill? EPCalibration automatically minimizes RMSE and |NMBE| of that dataset together, so the search stays multi-objective.

Guideline 14 metrics

RMSE, NMBE, CV(RMSE), maximum difference, and the share of hours deviating by more than 1°C — reported live and logged per generation.

Tag-based IDF control

##tag## substitution gives direct control over any EnergyPlus parameter, with automatic injection for the ten most common calibration parameters.

Two-stage refinement

Optional second run that narrows ranges around the balanced solution (with halved steps) or re-runs seeded with the elite Pareto front.

Fast & reproducible

Halton low-discrepancy sampling, parameter step grids, evaluation caching and parallel simulations — plus full CSV logs of every run.

FAQ

Common questions

Can I try EPCalibration before buying?

The installer is free to download, so you can confirm it installs and launches on your machine. Running calibrations requires an activated license key.

What do I need besides EPCalibration?

A working EnergyPlus installation (free from energyplus.net, v9.4 or newer), your IDF model and weather file, and measured data as CSV — from a monitoring system, data logger, or monthly utility bills.

How is the license key delivered?

After your PayPal payment is confirmed, your license key is sent to the email address associated with your PayPal account — within 24 hours, usually much sooner. If it doesn't arrive, check your spam folder, then email architecture.optimization@gmail.com with your PayPal transaction ID.

Can I move my license to a new computer?

Each key activates on one machine. If you replace your computer, email architecture.optimization@gmail.com with your license key and the license can be reset for activation on the new machine.

What happens after one year?

The license is valid for one year from activation. After expiry, the software prompts for a new key — renewing is the same one-time purchase, with no automatic billing.

Does EPCalibration need an internet connection?

Only briefly: once for activation, and occasionally to re-verify the license. All simulations and calibrations run entirely on your own machine — your models and measured data never leave your computer.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. EPCalibration is a desktop application with a full graphical interface. You tag parameters, load measured data, and set objectives in the GUI; the EnergyPlus runs and NSGA-II optimization are fully automated.

How is this different from ARCHOPT?

ARCHOPT optimizes new building designs (energy, carbon, comfort). EPCalibration tunes an existing EnergyPlus model so it matches measured reality — the essential first step before retrofit studies, M&V, or research. They work great together, and each has its own license.
